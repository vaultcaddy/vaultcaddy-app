1. Why Can't I Copy Text from PDF Bank Statements?

If you've tried to copy text from a PDF bank statement and it doesn't work, you're not alone. This is one of the most common frustrations for accountants and business owners.

The Root Cause

Your PDF is likely an image-based PDF (also called "scanned PDF" or "image PDF"), not a text-based PDF.

What this means:
‚Ä¢ The PDF doesn't contain actual text data
‚Ä¢ It's just a picture of text (like a photograph)
‚Ä¢ Your computer sees it as an image, not text
‚Ä¢ Copy-paste doesn't work because there's no text to copy

Why Hong Kong banks do this:
‚Ä¢ 90% of HK bank PDFs downloaded from online banking are image-based
‚Ä¢ Security concern (harder to edit/forge)
‚Ä¢ Legacy systems using scanned documents
‚Ä¢ Banks: HSBC, Hang Seng, Bank of China, DBS, etc.

7. How to Test Your PDF Type

Here are 3 simple tests to determine if your PDF is image-based:

Test 1: Text Selection Test

  1. Open your PDF
  2. Try to select a single word with your cursor
  3. If you can select individual words: Text-based PDF ‚úì
  4. If you can only select entire pages: Image-based PDF ‚úó

Test 2: Search Function Test

  1. Open your PDF
  2. Press Ctrl+F (Windows) or Cmd+F (Mac)
  3. Search for a word you can see in the PDF
  4. If search finds the word: Text-based PDF ‚úì
  5. If search finds nothing: Image-based PDF ‚úó

Test 3: Zoom Test

  1. Open your PDF
  2. Zoom in to 200-300%
  3. If text stays crisp and clear: Text-based PDF ‚úì
  4. If text becomes blurry or pixelated: Image-based PDF ‚úó
